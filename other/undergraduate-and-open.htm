Undergraduate and Open Categories

RoboGames 2011 Undergraduate and Open category competitions are intended to the inspired university students and any other team who can face to the challenge of developing sophisticated robot to play "Robotic Soccer" . This robot might be able to throw the ball in to goal and defend its own goal by processing a video stream or using any other sensor. Winning Robots will be awarded with superior prizes and valuable certificate issued by IESL. Every participant will be appreciated with a participation certificate issued by IESL.

Team


Participating teams can have maximum of five members.

Every Undergraduate Category team member must be an undergraduate university student currently learning in a university within Sri Lanka.

Open category is open for everyone.

One team can only have one robot in the competition.

Every person must be only in one team.


Competition


Final competition will be held in RoboGames 2011 stall in National Engineering Exhibition, "Techno 2011" which will be held in 7th, 8th and 9th October 2011 at Sirimavo Bandaranayaka Exhibition Center from 10a.m. onwards.


Undergraduate category tournaments will be having first, and competent undergraduate category teams can advance to the open category with the approval of judge panel.


Challenge


The task for the undergraduate and open categories is to build a robot that can play "Robotic Soccer". Robot can communicate with a personnel computer in any wireless media to get automated instructions, and have hit or throw the ball in to the opponent goal while staying in own side. It may also have to avoid opponent robot by protecting its goal.




Rules, Regulations and Guidelines


At the beginning a team will be selected to start the game by a toss.

Opponent team has to place their robot before the selected team by the toss.

The ball will be handed over to the selected team to begin the game.

Robot can navigate within only its side.

Fence around the play area can be used to direct the ball as far as the ball stays within the play area.

In a case of ball went away from the play area over the fence or getting a robot stuck, the game has to be start over with current score, and first strike will be given to the opposite side.

Maximum time a robot player is allowed to keep the ball in its side is 90 seconds. After this time limit, the strike is given to the other side.

A robot has to play 7 minutes in one side and another 7 minutes in the other side after a small interval.

Scored goals will be taken as the score.

Due to any reason if the robot has to be restarted, the strike will be given to the other party.

If two teams have scored equally at the end of the time duration, winner will be decided by the round of penalty shootouts, without goal keeping.

Any kind of sensors, actuators or electronic equipment can be used within the given dimensions.

External power supplies are not allowed.

Video feed of the top view of play area will be provided to the PCs. PC can only communicate with robot through any kind of wireless media.

Robot must have flat surface on top of it. Judges will provide a green or blue colored sticker with the shape of a triangle for each match to paste on top of the robot.  (As shown below)

 

These rules can be changed with decision of the judges.
